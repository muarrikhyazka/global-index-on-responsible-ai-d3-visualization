Global Index on Responsible AI
Beastly Challenges in Responsible AI and Where to Find Them

Explore insights into the challenges we face in living responsibly with artificial intelligence, or AI. There are 10 key challenges, developed from the Top 10 Takeaways of the Global Index on Responsible AI (GIRAI) 2024. This visualization provides a comprehensive look at how these challenges are distributed across the globe. The visualization highlights the complexity and diversity of issues countries face as they navigate the ever-changing landscape of AI ethics and governance. From a global perspective, it provides meaningful insights into the obstacles nations are facing and how they should ensure responsible AI development by mapping these issues.

10 Key Challenges
Each Issues Has Its Own Characteristics
Issue 1 - Responsible AI Governance : No Government Framework in National AI Policy
Issue 2 - Government Framework for Protecting Human Right : No Government Framework for Access to Remedy and Redress, No Government Framework for Impact Assessments, No Government Framework for Public Procurement
Issue 3 - International Cooperation in Responsible AI : No Framework adressing the International Cooperation, No evidence of non-state actors working towards International Cooperation
Issue 4 - Responsible AI and Gender : No Government Framework, No Government Action, No NSA
Issue 5 - Inclusion and Equality : No Evidence in Bias and Unfair Discrimination Areas, No Evidence in Children's Rights, No Evidence in Cultural and Linguistic Diversity, No Evidence in Gender Equality, No Evidence in Labour Protection and Right to Work
Issue 6 - Labor Protection from Government : No Government Framework, No Government Action
Issue 7 - Cultural and Linguistic : No Government Framework, No Government Action, No NSA
Issue 8 - Government Led-Activity on Safety, Security and Reliability : No Framework, No Action
Issue 9 - Crucial Role of Non-State Actors : No Evidence of Initiatives by Non-State Actors
Issue 10 - Long Way to Go : Scored up to only 25 points out of 100 in the GIRAI
